Unless otherwise agreed between the Parties, the agreed Discharge rate is 2,000 metric tonnes per ‘weather working day’ SSHEX.

The Buyer shall guarantee to berth and discharge the vessel without undue delay when the vessel arrives at discharge port. The Buyer to ensure all documentation and import procedures completed prior to vessel’s arrival. The performing Vessel to tender NOR anytime day or night 24 hours SHINC to Buyer’s nominated vessel agent on arrival at anchorage WIBON, WIPON, WIFPON, WICCON.

6.5 Laytime

  1. The laytime calculation shall be based on the discharge rate mentioned in Clause 6.3.
  2. Time to count from 1300 hrs if NOR is tendered before or at noon (1200), and from 0800 hrs next working day if NOR is tendered afternoon, unless earlier commenced, whether in berth or not, whether in free pratique or not, whether custom cleared or not.
  3. Time shall not count from 17:00 Hrs on Saturday to 08:00 Hrs on the Monday even if used unless the vessel is already on demurrage
  4. For the purposes of this Clause 6.5, the laytime shall not include:
    1. time expended:
      1. shifting the Vessel from anchorage to the berth at the Discharge Port;
      2. preparing any draft surveys; or
      3. Inspection on vessel’s holds cleanliness by surveyor shall count as laytime, but if the vessel fails to pass the hold inspection, all the time loss until pass the re-inspection shall not count as laytime.
    2. time lost:
      1. due to the breakdown of the Vessel’s cranes, winches, motive power on prorate basis for number of cranes out of order;
      2. which is attributable to the unsuitability of the Vessel; or
      3. which is due to the actions or inactions of the Seller and/or the Vessel’s staff.
  5. Laytime counting ceases with the completion of discharge of the Vessel.
  6. Once on demurrage always on demurrage clause to apply.

6.6 Despatch and Demurrage

  1. If the time used in discharging the Product exceeds the laytime allowed for discharging in accordance with Clause 6.5, the Buyer shall be liable for demurrage to the Seller as set out in vessel nomination for such excess time.
  2. If the Buyer is able to discharge the Product in less time than the laytime allowed in accordance with Clause 6.5, the Seller shall be liable to the Buyer for dispatch which shall be 50% of the demurrage rate as set out in vessel nomination.
  3. Any demurrage or dispatch shall be accepted and paid within 30 days after the date of receipt of an invoice/debit note from the Party to whom such payment is due, setting out the relevant laytime calculations unless disputed by the other Party in good faith.

6.7 Overtime and ship agent

Overtime costs are to be paid on the following basis:

  2. overtime ordered by port authorities shall be for the account of the Buyer; and
