Notice of
Readiness (NOR)		 Upon arrival within the port limits, the vessel will tender notice of readiness (NOR) to Buyer or Buyer's nominee whether in port or not (WIPON), whether in berth or not (WIBON), whether in free pratique or not (WIFPON), whether customs cleared or not (WCCON) but only during official working hours [i.e. 0800 to 1700 hrs on week days-Monday to Friday and 0800 to 1200 hrs on Saturday] of the port. NOR shall not be tendered on CP holiday also. NOR can be tendered either in writing or email or fax.

If upon entering the Port, the vessel fails to obtain free pratique or for any other reason due to fault of vessel found unfit to discharge the cargo except the case due to failure of Buyer, then time shall cease to count as laytime until the vessel is fully ready to proceed for discharge. In such a case, NOR shall be retendered once the vessel is completely ready in all aspects.
Laytime: Laytime shall commence 12 (Twelve) Hrs after tendering of valid NOR during official working hours of the Discharge port.

Time shall not count between 00:01 Hrs of Sunday or legal/CP holiday and 00:01 Hrs on the first working day thereafter even if used unless the vessel is already on demurrage.

Time taken for steaming from anchorage to berth not to count as laytime unless vessel on already on demurrage. Time taken for shifting between berths shall count as laytime and cost/ expense of shifting will be for Buyer's account.

Overtime to be for account of the party ordering same, but crew overtime always to be for Seller / owner's account.

Once on demurrage, always on demurrage to apply, except where time is lost on account of reasons attributable to vessel.

Buyers have the right to work during excepted periods, such time used not to count as laytime unless vessel is on demurrage. Laytime shall cease to count on completion of discharge.
First opening and last closing of hatches to be for owner's /Seller's account and time used not to count as lay time. The vessel should have hatch covers which can be easily closed without loss of time and the vessel should not have Pontoon hatch covers. The Buyer shall not be liable for any delay caused due to any of the following reasons namely: strike, lockout, stoppage or restraint of labor for Master / Officers and crew of the vessel, fire, explosion, storm, short landing due to reasons attributable to the vessel or any other force majeure circumstances.

However, when delay is caused to vessel getting into berth after giving NOR, for any reason over which Buyer has no control, the vessel not conforming to the limitations / regulations at the discharge port, etc., such delay will not count as used lay time.

All time lost, stoppage of cargo discharge operations, cost & expenses arising due to breakdown of the Vessel's cranes / winches / machines or equipment's of Vessel or motive power or any other reasons attributable to the vessel, if the vessel is pulled out of berth on account of Safety by the Port Authorities shall be to Ship Owner's account and such period shall not be counted as lay time.

Vessel Owners/ crew to adhere to and comply with all covid-19 quarantine regulations, if any on arrival at disport. Valid NOR can only be tendered/accepted once any quarantine period has concluded at the discharging port.

If vessel is placed in quarantine by health authorities for crew health reasons, lay time not to start to count until clearance is given by the concerned authorities up to commencement of cargo operations.

Time used for Cargo damage inspection preventing cargo discharge operations as well as draft survey performance by Surveyors not to count as laytime.
