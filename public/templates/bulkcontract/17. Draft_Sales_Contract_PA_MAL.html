Results of this control sample based on the composite sample of load port and unloading port shall be binding on both parties. In case the buyer or seller is not able to send his sample for testing directly to the referee lab within 30 days from the date of communication regarding the difference in quality, then the results of analysis based on the available port sample to be binding on both parties.   8. The method of analysis of P2O5 shall be the ISO 3706.

Force majeure:

Should either party be wholly or partially prevented from carrying out its obligations under the Contract (other than the obligations to pay money when due) due to circumstances beyond the reasonable control of the party (“Force Majeure Event”), including without limitation, (i) acts of God, acts of governmental authorities, acts or omissions of governmental authorities or epidemics, pandemics, fire, explosion, floods or fires, floods, atmospheric disturbances, explosions, lightning, storms, typhoons, tornados, earthquakes, landslides, soil erosion or subsidence, washouts, hurricanes; (ii) wars, riots, civil wars, blockades, insurrections, sabotage, acts of public enemies or civil disturbances; (iii) orders, judgments, rulings, decisions or other acts of any governmental, civil or military authority; (iv) boycotts, strikes, lockouts or other similar industrial disturbances; (v) any other acts or events whatsoever not within the control of the Seller which the Seller shall not be able to prevent, avoid, resist or overcome, the obligations which cannot be carried out shall be suspended as long as the Force Majeure Event prevails. The party invoking Force Majeure Event shall promptly so notify within two working days the other party in writing. The notice must (a) specify the obligations it cannot perform, (b) describe the event of Force Majeure Event, (c) estimate the time during which the Force Majeure Event will continue; and (d) specify the measures proposed to remedy the Force Majeure Event

In the event that the Force Majeure Event shall prevail for more than ninety (90) days from the date of its commencement, then either party shall be entitled to cancel the Contract by written notice to the other party, without prejudice to any of the rights of either party accrued prior to the termination date.

The provisions in the above Clauses notwithstanding, responsibility for a vessel’s cancellation charges, if any, shall be for account of the party invoking the Force Majeure Event, if the Force Majeure Event commences after a vessel has been fixed and/or a vessel has arrived at the loading port (whether or not loading has commenced).

Certificate issued by the Chamber of Commerce or any equivalent approved agency in the country of the Seller/Buyer shall be sufficient proof of the existence of the above circumstances and their duration. Buyer recognizes seller is not the producer of the cargo and any Force Majeure condition affecting the producer of the cargo shall constitute as the Force Majeure condition affecting the seller under this contract. It has been expressly understood that once loading is commenced, force majeure is not applicable.

  3. In case of breach by either party, the party not in breach will issue 30 days notice in writing to the party in breach to rectify the breach. If the breaching party fails to rectify the breach within the notice period, the Contract shall stand terminated.

If the Contract is thus terminated, the Parties shall settle all their accounts and all provisions and terms of the Contract including payment obligations shall be applicable on the agreed shipment(s) of any quantity loaded, supplied or shipped up to the termination date of the Contract.
